# 📊 Limit Order Book Simulation with Agent-Based Modelling

## Limit Order Book (LOB) Environment

We simulate a **continuous double-auction limit order book (LOB)** where agents submit buy/sell limit orders.

### 🔁 Matching Rules

- Buy executes if:

$$
P_{\text{buy}} \geq \text{best ask}
$$

- Sell executes if:

$$
P_{\text{sell}} \leq \text{best bid}
$$

### 📈 Mid Price

$$
M_t = \frac{\text{best bid}_t + \text{best ask}_t}{2}
$$

## Zero-Intelligence Traders (ZIT)

ZIT agents follow **random but rational (no-loss) rules**.

### 📐 Order Generation

Buyers:

$$
v_i \sim U(P_f, P_f + S), \quad b_i \sim U(P_{\min}, v_i)
$$

Sellers:

$$
c_i \sim U(P_f - S, P_f), \quad a_i \sim U(c_i, P_{\max})
$$

✔ No optimization  
✔ No learning  
✔ Pure randomness under constraints  

---

## Behavioural Traders (BT)

Behavioural traders form expectations using **fundamental + technical + noise signals**.

---

## 📐 Expected Return Model (Core Formula)

$$
r^{t}_{e,j} =
\frac{1}{\sum_i w_{i,j}}
\left(
w_{1,j} \log \frac{P_f}{P^{t-1}} +
w_{2,j} \log \frac{P^{t-1}}{P^{t-\tau_j-1}} +
w_{3,j} \epsilon^t_j
\right)
$$

---

## 🔍 Explanation of Each Term

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| $j$ | Agent index |
| $t$ | Time step |
| $P_f$ | Fundamental price |
| $P^{t-1}$ | Current market price |
| $\tau_j$ | Lookback window |
| $\epsilon^t_j$ | Noise |
| $w_{1,j}$ | Fundamental weight |
| $w_{2,j}$ | Technical weight |
| $w_{3,j}$ | Noise weight |

---

## 🧠 Intuition Behind the Formula

### 1. Fundamental Component

$$
\log \frac{P_f}{P^{t-1}}
$$

- If price < fundamental → **buy signal**
- If price > fundamental → **sell signal**

---

### 2. Technical (Momentum) Component

$$
\log \frac{P^{t-1}}{P^{t-\tau_j-1}}
$$

- Captures **trend-following behavior**

---

### 3. Noise Component

$$
\epsilon^t_j \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_\epsilon)
$$

- Represents randomness

---

### ⚖️ Normalization

$$
\frac{1}{\sum_i w_{i,j}}
$$

---

## 📈 Expected Price

$$
P^t_{e,j} = P^{t-1} \cdot \exp(r^t_{e,j})
$$

## Return Construction

$$
r_k = \ln \left( \frac{M_{B+100k}}{M_{B+100(k-1)}} \right)
$$

---

## 📊 Stylized Facts

### ✔ Excess Kurtosis

Measures fat tails

---

### ✔ Volatility Clustering

$$
\text{ACF}_\ell = \text{Corr}(r_t^2, r_{t-\ell}^2)
$$

for lags $\ell = 1, \dots, 6$

## 📊 Comparison with Real Market Data

To evaluate the realism of the simulated market, we compare the statistical properties of returns generated by:

- Zero-Intelligence Traders (**ZIT**)
- Behavioural Traders (**BT**)
- Real market data (**S&P 500**)

### 📈 Stylized Facts Comparison

| Metric | ZIT | BT | S&P 500 |
|-------|-----|----|--------|
| ACF Lag 1 | 0.20 | 0.12 | 0.30 |
| ACF Lag 2 | 0.08 | 0.08 | 0.28 |
| ACF Lag 3 | 0.07 | 0.06 | 0.24 |
| ACF Lag 4 | 0.07 | 0.06 | 0.24 |
| ACF Lag 5 | 0.08 | 0.04 | 0.12 |
| ACF Lag 6 | 0.05 | 0.05 | 0.15 |
| Excess Kurtosis | 1.04 | 3.86 | 2.91 |

---

### 🔍 Key Insights

- **Volatility Clustering**  
  The S&P 500 exhibits strong autocorrelation in squared returns.  
  - ZIT partially captures this effect  
  - BT produces more stable but slightly lower persistence  

- **Fat Tails (Kurtosis)**  
  - ZIT underestimates tail risk (**1.04**)  
  - BT significantly improves realism (**3.86**)  
  - Close to empirical value (**2.91**)  

- **Model Realism**  
  - ZIT provides a basic benchmark with limited realism  
  - BT better captures key stylized facts of financial markets  

---

### 🧠 Interpretation

These results highlight that:

- Simple random behaviour (ZIT) is not sufficient to reproduce real market dynamics  
- Incorporating **behavioural components** (fundamental + technical + noise) significantly improves realism  
- Agent heterogeneity and expectation formation are essential for generating **fat tails and volatility clustering**

---

### 🚀 Takeaway

The behavioural agent model provides a **closer approximation to real financial markets**, demonstrating the importance of:

- Feedback between price and expectations  
- Non-linear interactions between agents  
- Adaptive and heterogeneous trading behaviour
